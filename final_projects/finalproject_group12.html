Question 2

Question: Which right (human right) as an aggregate had the highest rate of increase in the United States?

For this problem, I have decided to limit my scope and view the data from 2010 - 2021, since these were also that years that had the most consistent data collection, and also had the data for the metric we were observing. For this question, I looked at the indicators for the following rights:

  • LMY_QofL_All_GBScore (Queality of Life)
  • LMY_Education_All_GBScore (Education)
  • LMY_Food_All_GBScore (Food)
  • LMY_Health_All_GBScore (Health)
  • LMY_Housing_All_GBScore (Housing)
  • LMY_Work_All_GBScore (Work)

The GB score metric was used because it scored the rights aspect measured on the percentage of the global best benchmark, which was optimal in helping us understand the rate of increase in the United States. The data was collected from the Human Rights Measurement Initiative (HRMI) and was used to measure the rate of increase in the United States for each of these rights.

The right that had the highest rate of increase was the right to work, which had an average annual change of 0.4% from 2010 to 2021. This shows that the right to work is becoming more prevalent in the United States, while the right to health is declining.
